# EU4 2K European National Ideas Review

## Scope

This review covers the remaining playable European countries after Serbia, Germany, France, Britain and Russia were handled separately. It includes the South Caucasus and Turkey because ET places them directly in the modern European political system. No gameplay files were changed.

Microstates without independent ET tags or province representation cannot receive normal national ideas. Andorra, Liechtenstein, Monaco and San Marino fall into this category. The Holy See is represented by `PAP`.

## Assignment overview

### Countries with tag-specific ideas

Albania, Armenia, Austria, Azerbaijan, Belgium, Belarus, Bulgaria, Croatia, Cyprus, Denmark, Estonia, Finland, Georgia, Hungary, Iceland, Ireland, Italy, Latvia, Lithuania, Luxembourg, North Macedonia, Moldova, Montenegro, Netherlands, Poland, Portugal, Romania, Slovakia, Slovenia, Spain, Sweden, Switzerland, Turkey, Ukraine and Yugoslavia all have tag-specific or direct culture-specific sets.

### Countries without a tag-specific set

## Missing-country design directions

### Bosnia and Herzegovina

Federal compromise, difficult terrain, plural society, reconstruction, regional diplomacy, defence industry and diaspora.

### Czech Republic

Industrial engineering, technical education, parliamentary institutions, central European trade, automotive production, cultural diplomacy and professional defence.

### Greece

Merchant shipping, tourism, maritime security, democratic institutions, diaspora, eastern Mediterranean diplomacy and difficult terrain.

### Malta

Strategic harbor, maritime services, financial administration, neutrality, tourism, Mediterranean diplomacy and compact defence.

### Kosovo

International support, young population, diaspora remittances, state building, difficult terrain, regional trade and defensive security.

## Balance outliers

The most urgent numerical outliers are:

- Italy: -25% core creation and +33% manpower.
- Poland: +30% cavalry power, +25% manpower and +15% morale.
- Yugoslavia: +25% manpower, +20% force limit, +15% morale and +15% goods.
- Denmark and Netherlands: +50% naval force limit.
- Cyprus: +33% naval force limit and +4 heretic tolerance.
- Moldova: -20% construction and -10% development cost.
- Slovakia: -2 unrest, -5% autonomy and -10% technology cost.
- Latvia: 15% infantry power, -20% maintenance, +20% recovery and +20% defence.

## Recommended implementation batches

### Batch 1 — Missing and focus-region countries

Bosnia and Herzegovina, Kosovo, North Macedonia, Montenegro, Albania, Croatia and Bulgaria. This gives the western Balkans complete modern coverage around Serbia.

### Batch 2 — Largest balance corrections

Italy, Poland, Spain, Yugoslavia, Denmark, Netherlands and Cyprus.

### Batch 3 — Central and eastern Europe

Austria, Czech Republic, Hungary, Romania, Moldova, Slovakia, Slovenia, Ukraine, Belarus and the Baltic states.

### Batch 4 — Northern and western smaller powers

Sweden, Norway, Finland, Iceland, Ireland, Belgium, Luxembourg, Switzerland and Portugal.

### Batch 5 — Eastern Mediterranean and Caucasus

Greece, Malta, Turkey, Armenia, Georgia and Azerbaijan.

### Batch 6 — Special cases

Holy See and any later-added microstates. Their mechanics should remain distinct rather than being forced into regional templates.

## Design rule

As with the completed major-power sets, replacements should use durable national capabilities relevant from 1836 onward. Avoid colonists, native policy, missionary strength, absolutism, dynastic succession, cavalry-specific bonuses and large core-creation discounts unless a country has a compelling gameplay need.

## Implemented in 1.6.0

- Added new modern unique sets for Bosnia and Herzegovina and Kosovo.
- Replaced the active sets for Albania, Croatia, Bulgaria, North Macedonia and Montenegro.
- Removed medieval dynasties, crusading themes, cavalry specialization, privateering and expansion-oriented core cost from these countries.
- Standardized the western Balkan sets around state capacity, terrain, infrastructure, trade, diplomacy, diaspora and professional defence.
